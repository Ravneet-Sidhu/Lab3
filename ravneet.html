India Gate

India Gate is a war memorial located in the heart of New Delhi, India. It was built in 1931 to honor the soldiers who died during World War I and in the Third Anglo-Afghan War. Standing at 42 meters tall, the gate is a symbol of national pride and sacrifice. The structure is made of red sandstone and granite, with inscriptions of soldiersâ€™ names etched on the walls. At the base of the monument is the Amar Jawan Jyoti, a flame that burns in memory of soldiers who have died in service to the nation.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an iconic mausoleum located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India. It was commissioned by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1632 in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth. Made from white marble and surrounded by lush gardens, the Taj Mahal is renowned for its stunning architecture, symmetrical layout, and intricate carvings. The central dome is flanked by four minarets, and the monument is situated along the banks of the Yamuna River.

Red Fort

The Red Fort (Lal Qila) is a historic fortification located in the city of Delhi. It was constructed between 1638 and 1648 by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for around 200 years. The fort is made of red sandstone, which gives it its name, and features grand structures like the Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audiences) and the Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audiences). The fort's architecture is a blend of Persian, Timurid, and Indian styles.
